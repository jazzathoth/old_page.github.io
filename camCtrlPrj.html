Camera Platform

Camera Control Platform Project Outline:

The plan for this project is to build a solar powered camera platform (or include a huge battery) to support an Arlo cellular internet security camera. This will be controlled by an STM32f1xx chip driving a stepper motor to rotate the camera through a 360deg arc in a single plane. This will be controlled through a sim800l gsm module. This will either work through occasional checkins using mosquito protocol, checkins to a completely separate server, or could be told to listen through an SMS message. In any case, it will retrieve a degree value from a remote server controlled through a webpage form consisting of two buttons that will increment the number clockwise or counterclockwise. This will be compared to the system's current state and the stepper will move in the appropriate direction until the numbers match. The platform will determine it's position from an arbitrary zero value on power on. The system will actively check in for a few minutes and will power down after a certain duration of inactivity from the server. This system is meant to be controlled from a smartphone operating in split screen mode with a browser in one half and the Arlo app in the other half of the screen. Its purpose is to position the camera to watch irrigation systems from a remote location to ensure everything is operating correctly.
